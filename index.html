SMART Goals Framework

The SMART framework helps in setting clear and achievable goals:

  • Specific: Goals should be clear and specific
  • Measurable: Include precise amounts and dates
  • Achievable: Goals should be realistic
  • Relevant: Align with your broader objectives
  • Time-bound: Have a deadline for completion

Session 3: How to Give an Impressive Introduction

March 5, 2025

Key Takeaways:

  • First Impression - Lasts only 7 seconds but creates lasting impact on interviewers.
  • Structure - Follow "Present → Past → Future" format (1-2 minutes max).
  • Present - Start with current status (education/internship).
  • Past - Highlight relevant academic/project experiences.
  • Future - Conclude with career goals aligned with company.
  • Body Language - Maintain eye contact, confident posture, and smile.
  • Voice Modulation - Speak clearly with varied pitch and pace.

Do's and Don'ts:

Do:
  • Practice with time limit
  • Tailor content to job role
  • Highlight unique strengths
Don't:
  • Memorize word-for-word
  • Share irrelevant personal details
  • Speak too fast or too slow

Action Items:

  • Prepare 3 versions of introduction (30 sec, 1 min, 2 min).
  • Record practice videos and analyze body language.
  • Get feedback from 2 peers/mentors.

Session 5: Types of Public Speaking

March 11, 2025

Key Formats:

Group Discussion (GD)
  • Purpose: Assess communication & teamwork
  • Duration: 15-20 minutes
  • Key Skills: Listening, building on points, consensus building
Relay Speaking
  • Purpose: Continue another speaker's thoughts
  • Duration: 1-2 minutes per speaker
  • Key Skills: Quick thinking, coherence, smooth transitions
Extempore
  • Purpose: Impromptu speaking on random topics
  • Duration: 2-5 minutes
  • Key Skills: Spontaneity, structuring thoughts quickly
Debate
  • Purpose: Present arguments for/against a motion
  • Duration: 3-7 minutes per speaker
  • Key Skills: Logical reasoning, rebuttals, evidence use

Common Success Tips:

  • Maintain eye contact with different audience members
  • Use hand gestures naturally to emphasize points
  • Practice pacing (not too fast/slow)
  • Record yourself to identify improvement areas

Session 7: Mastering Group Discussions

April 9, 2025

GD Structure:

Initiation (0-2 min)

Define topic scope with clear opening statement

Discussion (8-12 min)

Exchange ideas with logical arguments

Conclusion (2-3 min)

Summarize key points and consensus

Evaluation Criteria:

Content (40%)
  • Relevance
  • Depth
  • Factual accuracy
Communication (30%)
  • Clarity
  • Fluency
  • Body language
Behavior (30%)
  • Teamwork
  • Listening
  • Leadership

Pro Tips:

Initiate only if you have strong opening point

Build on others' points using "Yes, and..." technique

Use data/statistics to strengthen arguments

SWOC Analysis

Key Strategies:

  • Strength - Positive attribute, quality or skills that help us achieve our goals.
  • Weakness - Areas that need improvement or hold us back from performing at our best.
